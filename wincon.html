    Hyderabad Brothers

    The Hyderabad Brothers, D. Raghavachari and D. Seshachari, are a Carnatic music singing duo. Hyderabad Brothers are one among the most popular duo vocalists in Indian Classical Carnatic music. They are popularly known as Hyderabad brothers because of their long association with the city.

    Pichwai Temple Painting, Rajasthan

    Pichwai paintings are works of art that are used to adorn the walls of temples, behind the idol. The Pichwai style is from the Nathdwara School, and is identified by characteristic features of large eyes, broad nose and a heavy body, similar to the features on the idol of Shrinathji.

    Art & Craft
  • Intensive

    Sikki Grass Weaving, Bihar

    A haat bazaar in Sitamarhi district of Bihar is abuzz with villagers, children springing around the toy seller, plundering his basket, men hawking on newly fabricated tools brought by the blacksmith and the giggles of young women mixing with that of glass bangles. The craft of Sikki weaving has its root in traditional beliefs of Indian value system, yet it is a perfect example of sustainability in contemporary times.

    Art & Craft
  • Intensive

    Phad Painting

    Hues of the melting twilight blend into the silhouettes of curious listeners who have assembled around a balladeer under an anonymous tree in Rajasthan. Phad paintings illustrate the glory of Pabuji, a hero of the local tribe.

    Art & Craft
  • Intensive

    Mithila Painting, Bihar

    Janakpur is the place from where Raja Janak ruled the whole Mithila kingdom centuries ago. Being the birth place of some of the most celebrated folk takes of India, it comes out quite naturally that the natives of Mithila have an innate quality to conceive a visually captivating narrative art form, to tell these tales with their due reverence.These visual narratives are known as Madhubani Paintings, literally meaning the ‘Forest of honey’.

    Art & Craft
  • Intensive

    Patua Painting, West Bengal

    Patua Painting is a form of Scroll Painting indigenous to West Bengal, a state in India. They narrate mythological and historical stories and are a part of an elaborate cultural performance, where they are unrolled while the story accompanying the pictures is sung.

    Art & Craft
  • Intensive

    Cheriel Sawdust Masks, Andhra

    Cheriyal Scroll Painting is a stylized version of Nakashi art, rich in the local motifs peculiar to the Telangana. The main narrative is spiced up with scenes from simple rural life - Women doing chores in the kitchen, men working in the paddy fields or boozing away in merry abandon, festival scenes, etc. are endearingly depicted.

    Art & Craft
  • Intensive

    Warli Master Craftsman

    The Warlis live in Thane district of Maharashtra. Traditionally the women of the tribe created the wall paintings on their ‘chowks’ during marriages and festivals. These paintings were greatly influenced by the surroundings and day-to-day life of the Warli Tribe. The paintings are made by drawing geometric shapes like circles, triangles and squares.

    Art & Craft
  • Intensive

    Chittara Tribal Painting, Karnataka

    Chittaras are intricate wall paintings traditionally created by tribal women of malnad on their red-coated houses, as well as rangoli floor designs. Chittara paintings are a part of a larger context of creativity and celebration in the cultural milieu of the region.

    Art & Craft
  • Intensive

    Wood Caligraphy

    Caligraphy is the art of giving form to signs in an expressive, harmonious, and skillful manner. Ornamenting wood by this art to create priceless masterpeice is wood caligraphy.

    Art & Craft

    Buddhist Meditation

    Terracotta Pottery, Haryana

    Terracotta is the term normally used for sculpture made in earthenware, and also for various utilitarian uses including vessels (notably flower pots), water and waste water pipes, bricks, and surface embellishment in building construction, The term is also used to refer to the natural, brownish orange color, of most terracotta, which varies considerably.

    Art & Craft

    Bagru Block Printing

    Bagru is known for natural dyes and hand block printing. Bagru is the place of Raiger and Chhipa community. Chhipa community people who are involved in this printing tradition since 100 years ago and also Raiger community people are involved in processing and manufacturing of leather and their products (like boots, mochdi, Rajasthani jhutee and other leather goods).

    Art & Craft

    Gond Tribal Art

    The Gonds are among the largest tribes in Central India, numbering about 4 million. Gond art comes with the belief that a good image brings good luck. This inherent belief led the Gonds to decorate their houses and floors with traditional tattoos and motifs.

    Art & Craft

    Weaving, Assam

    Assam’s handloom industry is basically silk oriented. The salubrious climate of Assam is suitable for sericigenous flora and fauna. Four varieties of silk worms and their host-plants, mulberry, Eri, Muga and Oak.
